A Recipe for Gatah

  • Home
  • Gatah Recipe
Ingredients Notes
yeast powder either 2 packages of regular yeast or 2 1/4 teaspoons if you buy it in bulk
2 tablespoons of sugar
1/3 cup of lukewarm water body temperature too hot kills the yeast and you should heat the water in a kettle and then add cold water to cool it. Hot water from the tap isn't good to drink
one cup of milk to almost a boil
one stick of butter
one cup maple syrup
1 teaspoon of vanilla
2 eggs add the eggs last so that the other ingredients cool the milk down
About 2 pounds of flour I'll get back to you with how many cups that is!
Sesame Seeds
1 egg separate from the other two eggs, for brushing onto the dough

Instructions:

Proof the yeast. Combine the following in a bowl and let it sit for about 20 minutes so the yeast starts growing:

  • the yeast powder
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1/3 a cup of lukewarm water

Meanwhile, in a pan on the stove or in the microwave warm up:

  • one cup of milk to almost a boil

Add:

  • one stick of butter
  • one cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla
  • 2 eggs (add the eggs last so that the other ingredients cool the milk down)

Combine the proofed yeast and these ingredients. Then add flour. Stirring at first and then kneading it. If you go slowly you will be able to tell it is enough flour when your hands touching the dough stay clean and no more bits of dough hang on to your hands and no more flour can be incorporated.

Let it rise in a warm place until double in bulk.

Punch it down and knead it, let it rest for a few minutes then roll it out.

Cut the dough into 6 pieces. Roll each of them into a circle shape. Cover one half with cheese then fold over the other side so it looks like a calzone. Brush the top with egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Preheat the oven then bake at 375 for 20 or so minutes. 3 big stuffed Gatah fit onto one cookie sheet. Enjoy!
